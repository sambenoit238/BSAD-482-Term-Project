# Archetype Analysis #

## Archetype Mapping ##

One of the archetype structures that fits this decision is shifting the burden which is defined as a quick fix that undermines the capacity for fundemental solutions. Through this archetype, we know the problem is that people with disabilities have a lower unemployment rate than those without disabilities. To fix this issue quickly, the federal government could establish employment programs that incentivise organizations to hire more people with disabilities. While this might improve the employment levels for people with disabilities momenetarily as workplaces have money to make their offices a little more accessible for the short term, the problem itself isn't fixed entirely. Because of this quick fix, there might be some unintended consequences. These might include: the creation of temporary accomodations that make it difficult for people with disabilities to perform their jobs effectively, employees feel people with disabilities are hired only for DEIA reasons and not because of their skills or qualifications and employers only hiring those with disabilities if incentives are involved. To avoid these unintended consequences, employers could still be provided with an incentive to make their workplaces more accessible by the federal government, but also take courses or learn from those with disabilities why it's important to hire those with disabilities and the importance of making their workplaces more accessible to all. In short, because employers rely on incentives from the government to encourage hiring without having the education on inclusive practices, the system depends on these quick fixes. As a result people with disabilities may be hired for DEIA reasons rather than because of their skills and qualifications and they may not receive the accomodations needed to work effectively.
